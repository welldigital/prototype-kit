How your Well pharmacist can help with a long-term condition

Well Pharmacies in England offer free appointments with a pharmacist to help with any difficulties you might have with medicines you take for your long-term condition.

This can save you trips to your GP and waiting times for appointments.

What your Well Pharmacist can help with

Your Well Pharmacist can help with:

  • understanding the side effects of a new medicine and what to do about them
  • confusion about which medicine to take when
  • how to take your medicines to get the most out of them
  • amending which medicines you should take and how much
  • changing the type of pills you take if you find it hard to swallow them
  • any other worries you have about taking your medicines

Where necessary, your Well Pharmacist will talk to your GP to sort out a problem. You usually don’t need to go back and see your doctor.

You can talk to your Well Pharmacist about any problem or concern you have with your medicines. They’ll take the time to sort it out for you.

Who can benefit from a free medicines appointment

You’ll get the most out of a free appointment with your Well Pharmacist if you’re both of these:

1. You’re on a repeat prescription and either:

  • have been recently prescribed a new medicine
  • are on more than one medicine

2. You have a long-term condition, for example:

  • type 1 or 2 diabetes
  • heart condition, for example heart attack, high blood pressure, hypertension
  • lung condition (respiratory), for example asthma, emphysema, pulmonary fibrosis
  • depression

Making an appointment is easy

  1. Go to your local Well Pharmacy or phone them.
  2. Ask them for an appointment to talk about your new medicine and its side effects or to get help if you’re confused about the medicines you’re currently taking
  3. Go and see the pharmacist for your appointment. Take your medicines with you on.

What happens in the appointment

All Well Pharmacists are highly trained and will give you the best help possible with your medicines.

The appointment will be in a private consultation room at your local Well Pharmacy. It’s completely confidential.

Here is what happens:

  • the Well Pharmacist will ask you to sign a consent form
  • they will have a chat with you about your medicines
  • they will ask you about the medicines you currently take: side effects, how and when you take the medicines, any worries or difficulties you have
  • they will explain any medicine side effects for and what you could do about them
  • they will give you practical advice on how to take your medication and how to remember what to take when

You can ask any questions you have during the appointment. This is your time with the pharmacist.

How long the appointment lasts and what happens next

The appointment can last 10 to 45 minutes. It completely depends on your situation.

If your appointment is about a new medicine you’ve been prescribed, your Well Pharmacist may will follow up with a couple of phone appointments to see how you’re getting on.
