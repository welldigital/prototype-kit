How your Well pharmacist can help with your health condition

Well Pharmacies in England offer appointments with a pharmacist to help with any difficulties you have taking medicines to manage a health condition.

The service is free and can save you time visiting your GP and waiting for appointments as well as free up your GP's time.

How your Well Pharmacist can help

Your Well Pharmacist can help with:

  • understanding the side effects of a medicine and what to do about them
  • how and when to take your medicines to get the most out of them
  • explaining how your medicines actually help
  • organising alternatives to pills if you find them hard to swallow
  • any other worries you have about your medicines, for example how to use an inhaler

Where necessary, your Well Pharmacist will talk to your GP to sort out a problem. You usually don’t need to go back and see your doctor.

You can talk to your Well Pharmacist about any problem or concern you have with your medicines. They’ll take the time to sort it out for you.

Who can benefit from a medicine appointment

You'll get the most out of the medicine appointment if you use medicine to manage a health condition and start a new medicine or regularly take more than one.

It doesn't matter how old you are and the help is there for any health condition including asthma, COPD, high blood pressure, diabetes or a heart condition.

You can also walk into any Well Pharmacy and ask for help with your medicines.

What happens in the appointment

The appointment is a bit like a 'medicine check-up' and is:

  • free and confidential
  • takes place in a private consultation room in the pharmacy
  • lasts 10 to 45 minutes (it completely depends on your needs)

All Well Pharmacists are highly trained and will talk about your medicines and help with any worries or problems you have.

You can ask any questions you like (it’s your time with the pharmacist).

You’ll get the best help possible with your medicines.

After your appointment

If your appointment is about a new prescription medicine, your Well Pharmacist may follow up with a couple of phone calls to see how you’re getting on.
