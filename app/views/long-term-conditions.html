How your Well pharmacist can help with a long-term condition

Well Pharmacies in England offer free appointments with a pharmacist to help with any difficulties you might have with medicines you take for your long-term condition.

This can save you trips to your GP and waiting times for appointments.

What your Well Pharmacist can help with

Your Well Pharmacist can help with:

  • understanding the side effects of a medicine and what to do about them
  • how and when to take your medicines to get the most out of them
  • explaining how your medicines actually help
  • organising alternatives to pills if you find them hard to swallow
  • any other worries you have about your medicines, for example how to use an inhaler

Where necessary, your Well Pharmacist will talk to your GP to sort out a problem. You usually don’t need to go back and see your doctor.

You can talk to your Well Pharmacist about any problem or concern you have with your medicines. They’ll take the time to sort it out for you.

Who can benefit from a free medicines appointment

You’ll get the most out of a free medicines appointment if you have a long-term condition and are starting a new medicine or regularly taking more than one.

Long-term conditions include things like: asthma, COPD, high blood pressure, diabetes (type 1 or 2) or a heart condition.

Making an appointment is easy

  • 1

    Go to your local Well Pharmacy or phone them.

  • 2

    Ask them for an appointment to talk about your medicines.

  • 3

    Go to your appointment and take your medicines with you.

You can also walk into any Well Pharmacy and ask for help with your medicines.

What happens in the appointment

All Well Pharmacists are highly trained and will give you the best help possible with your medicines.

The appointment will be in a private consultation room at your local Well Pharmacy. It’s completely confidential.

Here is what happens, the pharmacist will:

  • have a chat with you about your medicines
  • ask you about the medicines you currently take: side effects, how and when you take the medicines, any worries or difficulties you have
  • explain any medicine side effects and what to do about them
  • they will explain any medicine side effects for and what you could do about them
  • give you practical advice on how to take your medication and how to remember what to take when

You can ask any questions you like during the appointment. It is your time with the pharmacist.

How long the appointment lasts and what happens next

The appointment can last 10 to 45 minutes. It completely depends on your situation.

If your appointment is about a new prescription medicine, your Well Pharmacist may follow up with a couple of phone calls to see how you’re getting on.
