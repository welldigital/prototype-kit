Prescription Costs

Paying for your medicines

How much you pay depends on the number of medicines you’re on and where you live. You might be exempt from paying all together.

NHS prescriptions

In England you have to pay £8.60 for each medicine you’ve been prescribed.

In Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland you don’t have to pay anything.

Private prescriptions

What you pay depends on the medicine you’re prescribed. To find out the cost, show your prescription.

Check if you can get free NHS prescriptions

The NHS offers free medicines for some people. You have to be one or more of the following.

Age

You’re:

  • 60 or over
  • 16 to 18 and in full-time education
  • 15 or under

Long-term condition, disability or pregnancy

Your GP will give you an application form to fill in. Within 10 days the NHS should send you a letter saying you don’t have to pay for your medicines. Show this to your local Well Pharmacy when you pick up your medicines.

If your GP didn’t give you an application form, phone the surgery to check your long-term condition or disability qualifies for an exemption.

Benefits

If you’re on benefits tell your GP. They’ll give you an application form to fill in.

Within 10 days the NHS should send you a letter saying you don’t have to pay for your medicines. Show this to your local Well Pharmacy when you pick up your medicines.

Tax credits

If you get tax credits the NHS automatically send you a letter saying you don’t have to pay for your medicines.

Think you shouldn’t be paying for your medicines and didn’t get a letter from the NHS?

Speak to your local Well pharmacist or call the NHS helpline on 0300 330 1349.

Save money on your NHS medicines

If you’re on 2 or more medicines a month you can pay for your prescriptions in advance and save at least £7.50 a month. It’s a bit like buying season ticket.

You’ll have to buy a ‘prescription prepayment certificate’ from the NHS. You can do this online.
